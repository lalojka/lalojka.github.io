Rapid Economic Growth (Early 1990s)

The affected Asian economies, including Thailand, South Korea, Indonesia, Malaysia, and others, experienced rapid economic growth in the early 1990s. This growth was fueled by foreign investments, particularly in real estate and infrastructure.

Current Account Deficits (Mid-1990s)

Current account deficits began to emerge in these countries as they imported more than they exported. This led to a reliance on foreign capital to finance the deficits.
